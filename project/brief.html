Project Brief - What will happen when we grow old?

Topic Summary

When thinking of mental health, the elderly is an unlikely age group to come to mind. However, it is important to note that they also suffer from mental illnesses, as 1 out of 5 Americans is projected to be an elder by year 2050 (US Census Bureau). Signs of depression in elders are often overlooked, despite occurring in 7% of the older population (World Health Organization). A main cause of this is the feelings of loneliness that 43 percent of elders have, which is often due to a lack of meaningful time spent with their loved ones (Botek). To solve this problem, it is important to make those family members aware of this problem. This project aims to produce an interactive game that allows the user to live a day through the perspective of a typical elder. By doing so, people are able to sympathize with the situations of older people and become more likely to spent time with elders in their family.

Target Audience

The target audience of this project would be middle-aged working adults. This target population has a regular 6-day working schedule, and has little time to relax. For an hour or two before bed, they tend to catch up with the new on the internet before they go to bed. Their salary allows them to own a house and afford necessary items. On their day off, they would go out with their family. However, they would typically leave their parents at home, as it is tiring for them to walk around for a long time. While they are able to provide their parents a comfortable life with their fairly good salary, they are reluctant to spent time with them as they are always on the go.

Goals of the Project

The main objective of this project is to bring awareness of the difficulties elders may face each day. An effective way to do so is to immerse oneself into their lives. The browser will act as the "eyes" of the user, as he or she navigates around a house to explore and perform activities for a set amount of time. The game will begin with a first-person conversation with family members. The user, playing the role of an elder, says his or her goodbyes to the family members as they set off to school or work. The user then proceeds to spent the rest the day doing activities in the house. The game will incorporate typical rooms for the user to navigate through, along with personal items that help create a narrative for the user's role as an elder whose spouse had recently passed away. For a more realistic experience. It will also incorporate the deteriorating physical abilities a typical elder would have, such as blurred vision, slower movements, and a shorter memory span. The mouse and keyboard will be used to navigate through the game.

Visual Strategies

To portray the real world more accurately, the general appearance of the game will be of vector images created based on real photos. To establish the atmosphere of the game, scenes with the family members will be shown brighter to represent happiness and companionship, while the scenes with the user alone will be darker and more faded to represent loneliness and isolation. To maintain consistency and legibility, a san serif font will be used, and text will appear at the bottom of the screen. The time can be seen at the top left of the screen to simulate the passage of time. To explain certain elements of the game to the user, a small text box will fade in consistently at the top right corner. Clickable items will be outlined when hovered over. When use of keyboard is necessary, instructions will appear. To create stimulate slow movement, a loading bar will appear whenever the user performs a task. Short memory span can be stimulated through a mini game in which the user needs to search for a phrase amongst other phrases.
