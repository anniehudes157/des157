Background Research

Experiences of the Elderly, Immigrants, and Disabled

For the final project, I am planning to create an interactive game that would give the users an idea of someone else’s live. My hope is that the will allow the users to gain a wider perspective of the world. Currently, I am deciding between depicting the lives of elders, immigrants, and people with disabilities. Therefore, I decided to research the difficulties that they may have in life.

In “Challenges Facing the Elderly”, the article mentions several difficulties that elders may be experiencing, but goes by unnoticed by the general public. One is the existence of “ageism”, in which people discriminate others based on their age. While many people treat the elderly in a kinder way due to their perceived fragility or their resemblance to their grandmother, there have also been cases where elderly women become the target of scams and robberies. Another major problem that is not often emphasized in media is the mistreatment of and abuse of elders. As the elderly become physically weaker and more dependent on others, the chances of them being abused increases. According to two US studies, one out of ten elders have indicated at least one type of elder abuse (Lumen Learning). This high, surprising fact increases the need to become aware of the problems elders face everyday.

To further understanding the mind of elderly people, I also read an academic study that explores the health and social problems of the Elderly. More than two hundred people participated for the study, contributing to test results that were quite unexpected results. The results indicated that the most common health problems for elders include hypertension, osteoarthritis, diabetes, or bronchial asthma. Cataract, anemia, and skin problems were also present in the survey takers. The study also discussed the fact that half of the subjects felt that they were neglected by their family, and more than one third felt that they were burdening their family. Despite the surprising results, it is also important to take not that this was a small sample of elders in India, which may or may not be different from the United States.

Besides the elderly, I also wanted to know more about the lives of immigrants in the United States, as I myself is an immigrant and have faced many challenges in the past as well. This issue is becoming more relevant as Donald Trump proposes to create a wall to stop illegal immigrants from crossing the border. From the article written by Christina Nuñez, it is revealed that the access to various services is a great challenge for immigrants, especially those who are undocumented. There is often a language barrier and stops the immigrants from getting the proper services that they need. This presents a large problem as many immigrants suffer from stress and mental health issues, but are unable to get the help they need. This is a greatly unfair situation as most immigrants intend to work hard in order to gain opportunities for their children to have better lives.

Another topic I wanted to explore was people with disabilities. An article by The Daily Dot explores the issues that people with disability are currently facing. As someone that usually does not pay a lot of attention to this group of people, the article was an eye-opening experience. One large problem that people with disabilities are facing is the lack of funding for the group, which will greatly affect their lifestyle. It was surprising to discover that disabled people are also, unfortunately, victims of police brutality. Other problems amongst people with disabilities include a large employment gap and the issue of being placed in special education due to their disabilities.

Research on the elderly, immigrants, and people with disabilities has given me a better understanding of what they are going through everyday. These ideas will help to further develop my final project as I intend to create an immersive experience that will stay true to the realistic problems that these groups of people face.
